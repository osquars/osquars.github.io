Vermintide

Vermintide is a co-operative action first person shooter and melee combat adventure set in the End Times of the iconic Warhammer Fantasy world.

Vermintide takes place in and around Ubersreik, a city overrun by Skaven. You will assume the role of one of five heroes, each featuring different play-styles, abilities, gear and personality. Working cooperatively, you must use their individual attributes to survive an apocalyptic invasion from the hordes of relentless rat-men, known as the Skaven. Battles will take place across a range of environments stretching from the top of the Magnus Tower to the bowels of the Under Empire.

The Map

The Map is where you can set up your party, select your mission and search for party. Only the one created a party (Host) can interact with the map if you are in party already.

  • You/Host(if in a party) can start missions
  • Choose mission and set difficulty
  • Advance settings like distance, hero filter, hosting and/or auto ready
  • See if friends are online to invite or join
  • Set the mission to private

Inventory

Forge

In our lovely home the Red Moon Inn we have a Forge. On this forge we can make stuff, upgrade, mould our items or destroy them.

On the Forge upgrading cost and salvage collect "shards". Every type of shards represents the rarity of the items.

  • White (Plentiful)
  • Green (Common)
  • Blue (Rare)
  • Orange (Exotic)

Fuse

  • Takes 5 items of the same rarity and fuse them to a new and better one.
  • You can only fuse items of the same rarity.
  • Fuse will have 20% probability for each weapon you add.
    • Example 1: 5 Common Great Hammers will give you 1 Rare Great Hammer
    • Example 2: 1 Common Crossbow, 2 Common Great Hammers and 2 Common Longbows will give you a (20%, 40%, 40% chance) Rare item.

Upgrade

  • Takes one common or higher item and unlock one passive trait.
  • Upgrading weapons by unlocking traits becomes more expensive as you upgrade your weapon.
  • First trait will cost 1 shard while the 2nd trait will cost 3 shards and the 3rd trait will cost 5 shards.
  • You can however choose freely which trait you want to get as the 1st and cheaper one.
    • Common traits require Plentiful shards
    • Rare traits require Common shards
    • Exotic traits require Rare shards

Salvage

  • Salvage every item you click on and make it to 1-3 shards of the same rarity.

You can only get items by the Loot Roll or level up your account.

Attack styles

Attack

All weapons have their way of hitting the enemies. Some can hit more than one enemy and some can easily damage heavier enemies like Stormvermin. While some will have problems with heavier enemies.

For melee there is everything from slashing, bashing, piercing, swinging and Stabbing. For ranged we have more of how hard it can hit, dispersion and range. Where magic have everything from missiles to Area of effect.

All melee weapons have a series of attack motions for both Normal and Charged attacks (varies from weapon to weapon). Max is 3. You can combine this attacks to get the full use of them.

Normal attacks is a tap with left mouse button. Charged attacks is holding down the left mouse button. But you might just need to hold it in for 0.5-1 sec. and not wait for the animation to be fully done.

Example - Dwarf Ranger:

  • Normal attacks - 1. Slice from right, 2. Slice from left, 3. Hack from top.
  • Charged attack - 1. Bash forward, 2. Swing from left, 3. Swing from right.
  • I might want to attack with Charged (1) first to get the skaven pushed back a little and get one with a Normal (2) hit and finish with a charged (3) to separate them one more time and even get a kill on it. This can give me hits on the skavens without them hitting me. Less damage on me and i can fight more before i need to heal and survive the mission.
  • Or it can be a horde attacking and you see the first skavens are coming one by one. You can then do the combo with Normal 1, Normal 2 and after that more than one skaven are just in front of you and you can finish it with C3 to push them off before you do a next combo or just attack with normal.

Block

Block and push can help you and your friends survive longer and to make more attacks. By splitting up the skavens makes it harder for them to hit you and you can attack easier an enemy that is by them self without get hit yourself.

Overcharge

When the Bright Wizard uses her magic she starts to fill her overcharged meter. when she get over 30%ish (notches in the meter) she will take longer to cooldown and you can start ventilate by using your reload button. It will ventilate only down to this notches. After that it will cooldown in normal speed. Bright wizard will cooldown as soon as she do not use the staff and when using the melee weapon.

We all want to kill skaven but we want to do it in our way to satisfy our self. To understand what weapon is best for you I have here something of a list for you to look on and see what can be a good weapon for you to use on your favorite character.

Weapon Stats

Damage

  • The higher bar you have the easier it is the kill with one blow.
  • Clan rats kills with one blow from around 1/2 of the bar (Various from mission difficulty).
  • Depending on rarity of the weapon you will get a higher damage boost. Damage boost varies from weapon types.

Attack speed

  • Hard to set a number on this.
  • Max is like almost instant while in the bottom we have maybe up to 2 sec.

Targets (how many targets you can hit)

  • See it as a cone in front of you, the higher bar you have the bigger the the cone are. Max is like 90 degree from you.
  • If you like to see with numbers I have split it up in 6 zones:
    • 1 target
    • 1-2 targets
    • 2-3 targets
    • 3-4 targets
    • 4-5 targets
    • 5-7 targets

Knockback (pushback on hits)

  • The Higher you have the more push/stun on the enemy you get on a hit.
  • Low bar is likely not to push/stun at all

Range

  • The distance is from just in front of you to almost the other side of a square (Thinking of the big square with the Sigmar statue in the magnus mission)

Special

Some weapons have special passive characteristics.

  • Armor penetration - Can penetrate heavy armor
  • Head shot - High damage when hitting the head
  • Burn - Small damage over time
  • Poison - Small damage over time

Stamina

  • All melee weapons have their stamina for blocking and pushing.
  • Push off cost two Stamina (one shield icon)
  • Higher mission difficulty makes blocking and pushing more important to use.

Traits

  • Passive traits that can be unlocked on the Forge (Red Moon Inn thread) from common and higher rarity weapons
  • Depending on rarity of the weapon will give you different amount of trait slots.
    • Common (1 Trait slot)
    • Rare (2 Trait slots)
    • Exotic (3 Trait slots)
  • List of traits can be found at my weapon and trinket traits thread.
